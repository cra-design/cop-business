When you need a BN

A business number (BN) is used as a standard identifier for a business operating in Canada. Your BN is used to interact with provincial, municipal, and other federal programs within Canada.

Why you need a BN

You may need a business number (BN) if you incorporate or your business activities require you to register for program accounts with the CRA. There is no cost when you register for a BN directly with the CRA.

A BN is required if you:

  • Need a GST/HST, payroll or another CRA program account
  • Incorporate your business
  • Interact with provincial, municipal or other federal programs
  • Become a federal government supplier
  • Want to manage your business [account] in your CRA account

If you already have a BN

Your business can only have one business number (BN). If you have previously registered for a BN, you cannot register for another one for the same business or legal entity.

If you need to register for CRA program accounts, you do not need to get a new BN if you already have one. Your existing BN is the standard identifier for your business and becomes a part of your CRA program account number.

If you do not know what your business number is or are not sure if you already have one, check options to find an existing BN.

Changes to a business that may require a new BN

There are certain situations or changes to your business which does not allow you to keep the same BN for your business. 

You may need to register for a new BN for any of the following:

  • Ownership changes for your business
  • Business structure changes (for example, incorporation)
  • New legal entity after a bankruptcy or receivership

For details: Changes to your business status

Registrations with a BN included

You can get a business number (BN) if you register for one directly with the CRA, but this is not the only way to get a BN. Depending on how you register, you may get a BN as a part of other provincial or federal registrations.

If you do not already have a BN, you will automatically get one if you:

  • Register for a CRA program account (like GST/HST or payroll)
  • Incorporate your business federally
  • Register or incorporate with certain provinces

Incorporate your business federally

You can incorporate your business federally for a fee through Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada (ISED). Once your federal incorporation is approved, you’ll automatically get a business number (BN) for your corporate income tax program account. You do not need to register separately with the CRA.

If you need other CRA program accounts like GST/HST or payroll, you can register for these accounts with the CRA after you get your BN.

For details: How to incorporate a business with ISED

Provincial registrations with a BN and program accounts included

If you are registering your business through certain provinces, you may be able to register for a federal BN and CRA program accounts at the same time. The registration process will be different for each province.

You can get a BN and CRA program accounts as a part of the provincial registration process for:

  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Manitoba
  • New Brunswick
  • Nova Scotia
  • Ontario
  • Prince Edward Island
  • Saskatchewan
To register for provincial programs

For provincial program registrations, you need to go directly to their respective websites to find out what provincial programs are available and how to register for them.

Provincial partner registration through BRO

You can use the Business Registration Online (BRO) service to connect to provincial program account registrations for Nova Scotia and Ontario. There may be costs for these provincial registrations.

Nova Scotia programs

You can use the BRO service to connect to Service Nova Scotia and apply for:

  • A business name with the Nova Scotia Joint Stock Companies
  • A Nova Scotia Workers' Compensation Board account
  • Various Nova Scotia licences and permits

For more information on doing business in Nova Scotia, go to Registry of Joint Stock Companies.

Ontario programs

You need to register your business name with the province of Ontario if you are not operating under your exact legal name. Ontario registration fees will apply. 

Use the BRO service to connect to the Ontario Business Registry with Service Ontario to:

  • Register or renew a business name with ServiceOntario
  • Apply for Ontario Employer Health Tax
  • Apply for Ontario Workplace Safety and Insurance Board

For more information on doing business in Ontario, go to ServiceOntario – Business services.
