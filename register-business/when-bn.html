When you need a BN

A business number (BN) is used as a standard identifier for a business operating in Canada. Your BN is used to interact with provincial, municipal, and other federal programs within Canada.

Why you need a BN

Certain business activities require you to register your business with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

You need a BN if you:

  • Register for any CRA program accounts (like GST/HST, or Payroll deductions)
  • Incorporate your business federally

When you register your business, you will get a unique 9-digit business number (BN) that identifies your business.

If you are not sure you have a business number or have forgotten it, there are options to find an existing BN.

Changes to a business that may require a new BN

A business or legal entity is allowed only one business number (BN).

You may need to register for a new BN for any of the following:

  • Ownership changes for your business
  • Business structure changes (for example, incorporation)
  • New legal entity after a bankruptcy or receivership

For details: Changes to your business status

Get a BN as part of other registrations

You will receive a BN if you:

  • Use Business Registration Online (BRO) to register
  • Add a CRA program account (like GST/HST or payroll)
  • Incorporate your business at the federal level

Provincial registrations with a BN included

These provinces automatically assign a BN and CRA program accounts when you register:

  • Alberta
  • British Columbia
  • Manitoba
  • New Brunswick
  • Nova Scotia
  • Ontario
  • Prince Edward Island
  • Saskatchewan

Incorporate federally

You can set up your business at the federal level through Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada. Once your incorporation is approved, you’ll automatically get a business number (BN) for corporate income tax. No need to register separately with the CRA.

If you need other CRA accounts (like GST/HST or Payroll), you can apply for them after you get your BN.

For details: Innovation, Science and Economic Development Canada.

Become a government supplier

If your business plans to sell goods or services to the Government of Canada, you must be a registered government supplier with a Procurement Business Number (PBN).

You must have a CRA business number before you can register for a PBN. Registration for a PBN is completed through CanadaBuys (formerly Buyandsell.gc.ca) which is managed by the Public Services and Procurement Canada.

The PBN allows your business to bid on tender opportunities for federal departments and agencies.

Your PBN is a 15-digit number which includes your CRA business number.

For PBN registration information, go to CanadaBuys (formerly Buyandsell.gc.ca).
