Steps for gathering what you need to register

Intro sentence.

  1. Get information all businesses need to register

    All businesses must provide the following personal and basic business information:

    • Last name
    • Social insurance number (SIN)
    • Date of birth
    • Personal postal code (where you live)
    • Business name
    • Business number (if this business already has one)
    • Type of business or organization (for example: sole proprietor, partnership, corporation, domestic worker, trust)
    • Name and SIN of owners as required
    • Physical address
    • Mailing address (if different from the physical address)
    • Description of major business activity

    If you are a representative, you will need to provide your RepID. For more information on representatives, go to Represent a Client.

  2. Confirm your business number (BN) if you have one

    If you don't have a BN, you will automatically get one as part of your registration.

    If you already have a BN

    You need to provide your existing 9-digit BN. If you do not know what your business number is or are not sure if you already have one, check options to find an existing BN.

    You will be asked to verify some information you used to register for your BN, including:

    • Business number
    • Business name
    • Business mailing and physical address including postal code
    • Owner's name and social insurance number (SIN)
    • Corporation's jurisdiction (if applicable)
    • Description of your major business activity

  3. Get additional information based on the program account you need

    GST/HST program account

    You must have all of the following on hand:

    • Amount of your annual worldwide and domestic GST/HST taxable income
    • Fiscal year-end
    • Reporting period

    If you are a non-resident, you will also need:

    • Type of products or services the business collects GST/HST on and supplier type (if applicable)
    • Filing type (simplified or normal)
    • Banking information
    • Information about Canadian employees (if applicable)
    • Address of location branch or office in Canada (if applicable)
    Payroll deductions program account

    You must have all of the following on hand:

    • Months of operation
    • Number of employees
    • Expected annual employee salaries
    • Date employees receive their first wages
    • Frequency and type of payments
    • Payroll service name (if any)
    • Affiliate, subsidiary, or franchise information (if applicable)
    Corporation income tax program account

    You must have all of the following on hand:

    • Operation type
    • Corporation name

    If you are a non-resident, you will also have to know if you will be filing certain special elections or special returns.

    Registered charity program account

    You must have all of the following on hand:

    • Type of account needed:
      • Charity
      • Canadian amateur athletic association (CAAA)
      • National arts service organization (NASO)
    Information returns program account

    You must know the type of account needed:

    • T5 group
    • Tax-free savings account (TFSA)
    • Contract payments
    • Compliance and exchange

    Non-residents may also need

    Additional types options:

    • T5018
    • Partnerships

    You must also know if you will be filing certain special elections or special returns.
