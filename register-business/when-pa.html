Understanding CRA program account numbers

A program account number consists of the:

  • 9-digit BN to identify the business
  • 2-letter program identifier code to identify the program account
  • 4-digit reference number to identify an individual program account (since businesses can have more than one of the same kind)

Each CRA program account has its own rules and requirements about when you need to register.

Registering for a CRA program account will get you a BN if you don't already have one. If you already have a BN, the program account will be added to your BN. Your business will only ever have one BN.

Example - GST/HST and payroll account numbers for your business

You start a sole proprietorship. Since you collect GST/HST from your clients, you will need a GST/HST program account. If you have one or more employees, you will also need a payroll deductions program account.

Once you've registered, your business will receive a:

  • 123456789 - Business number (BN)
  • 123456789 RT 0001 - GST/HST program account number
  • 123456789 RP 0001 - Payroll program account number

List of program accounts

The most common CRA program accounts a business may need are:

GST/HST

Program identifier: RT

There are many situations when you will have to register, such as:

  • Your business collects GST/HST and its revenue exceeds $30,000 within a calendar year
  • You are a taxi operator or ride-share driver

Find other situations when you need to register and start charging the GST/HST.

Payroll deductions

Program identifier: RP

You may need to register if you are:

  • An employer
  • A trustee
  • A payer of other amounts related to employment

For details: Determine if you need to register for a payroll account

Corporation income tax

Program identifier: RC

You may need to register if you want to:

  • Incorporate your business
  • Keep your business as a separate legal entity
  • Function with limited liability

For details: Corporation income tax program account

Information returns

Program identifier: RZ

You need to register if you are required to file certain information returns with the CRA, such as:

  • RRSP or PRPP
  • T5
  • T5018
  • TFSA
  • Partnership

For details: Information returns program account

Registered charities

Program identifier: RR

You need to register if you want to:

  • Become a registered charity
  • Issue official donation receipts

For details: Determine if you should apply for charitable registration

 

Excise and specialty tax program accounts

Intro sentence.

Underused housing tax account (RU)

Used when your corporation files a Underused Housing Tax return for each of their properties in Canada. If you are not a corporation, you don't need the RU program account to file this return.

For details: Underused Housing Tax account

Fuel charge account (CT)

Used for businesses that need to include a fuel charge on products.

For details: Fuel Charge account

Excise duty account (RD)

Used for businesses that need to charge excise duty on products.

For details: Excise duty account

Excise tax and special levies account (RE)

Used when your business is incorporated.

For details: Excise tax and special levies account

Air travelers security charge account (RG)

Used for air carriers that charge the air travellers security charge.

For details: Air travelers security charge account

Insurance premium tax account (RN)

Used for brokers and agents who place contracts of insurance.

For details: Insurance premium tax account

Luxury tax account (LT)

Used for businesses that need to charge luxury tax on the sale or importation of select luxury items.

For details: Luxury tax account

Digital services tax account (DT)

Used for businesses that have Canadian digital services revenue.

For details: Digital services tax account
