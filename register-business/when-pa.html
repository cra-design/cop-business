Understand your CRA program account number

Your CRA program account number is used to report your business activities to the CRA.

If you already have a business number (BN), you need to provide it when you register for a CRA program account. The program account is added to your existing BN to create your CRA program account number.

If you do not have already have a BN, you will automatically get a one when you register for a CRA program account.

A CRA program account number consists of:

  • Your unique 9-digit business number (BN)
  • A 2-letter program identifier to indicate the program account type
  • A 4-digit reference number to identify specfic program accounts if you have more than one of the same type
Example - Program account numbers for GST/HST and payroll

Kai started a business this year selling jewellery they've designed. Since sales will be close to $30,000 this year, they will have to start charging GST/HST. This means they will need to get a GST/HST account.

Since Kai does not have a business number, they will automatically get a business number when they register for a GST/HST account:

  • 123456789 - Business number (BN)
  • 123456789 RT 0001 - GST/HST program account number

A few months later, Kai wants to hire someone to help out at the store. Since Kai already received a BN when he registered for a GST/HST account, the same BN can be used to register for a payroll deductions account:

  • 123456789 RP 0001 - Payroll program account number

When you need a CRA program account

Your business must register for CRA program accounts if you conduct certain business activities that need to be reported to the CRA. These activities may include if you collect GST/HST, hire employees, or incorporate your business.

Each CRA program account has its own registration and reporting requirements. You must review these to determine what you need for your business and your obligations once you've registered.

CRA program accounts

CRA program accounts you may need to register for:

GST/HST (RT)

There are many situations when you will have to register, such as:

  • Your business collects GST/HST and its revenue exceeds $30,000 within a calendar year
  • You are a taxi operator or ride-share driver

Find other situations when you need to register and start charging the GST/HST.

Payroll deductions (RP)

You may need to register if you are:

  • An employer
  • A trustee
  • A payer of other amounts related to employment

For details: Determine if you need to register for a payroll account

Corporation income tax (RC)

You may need to register if you want to:

  • Incorporate your business
  • Keep your business as a separate legal entity
  • Function with limited liability

For details: Corporation income tax program account

Information returns (RZ)

You need to register if you are required to file certain information returns with the CRA, such as:

  • RRSP or PRPP
  • T5
  • T5018
  • TFSA
  • Partnership

For details: Information returns program account

Registered charities (RR)

You need to register if you want to:

  • Become a registered charity
  • Issue official donation receipts

For details: Determine if you should apply for charitable registration

 

Excise and specialty tax program accounts

Intro sentence.

Underused housing tax account (RU)

You need to register if you:

  • Must file an Underused Housing Tax (UHT) return for properties in Canada

For details: Who must file a return and pay the tax

Fuel charge account (CT)

You may need to register if your business includes a fuel charge on products and you are one of the following:

  • Producer
  • Distributor
  • Importer
  • Emitter
  • User of fuel or combustible waste
  • Marine, air, rail or road carrier

Find out a full listing: Fuel charge registration

Excise duty account (RD)

You need to register if your business charges excise duties on products such as:

  • Beer products
  • Cannabis
  • Spirits
  • Tobacco
  • Vaping products
  • Wine

For details: Excise duties technical information

Excise tax and special levies account (RE)

You need to register if your business charges excise taxes on certain products such as:

  • Petroleum products
  • Fuel-inefficient vehicles
  • Air conditioners designed for use in automobiles, station wagons, vans, or trucks

For details: Excise taxes and other levies technical information

Air travelers security charge account (RG)

You need to register if your business include services such as:

  • Air transportation of people on an aircraft (maximum take-off weight of more 2,730 kg)
  • Chargeable enplanements

For details: Air travellers security charge program account

Insurance premium tax account (RN)

You may need to register if you are:

  • Acting as a broker or agent who places or assists in obtaining or placing a contract of insurance 

For details: Insurance premiums tax program account

Luxury tax account (LT)

You may need to register with the Canada Revenue Agency if you sell or import select luxury items such as:

  • Certain vehicles and aircraft priced over $100,000
  • Certain vessels priced over $250,000

For details: Luxury tax registration

Digital services tax account (DT)

A foreign or domestic businesses must register if their:

  • Canadian digital services revenue was greater than nil in the calendar year
  • Total revenue was at least €750 million during a fiscal year that ended in the prior calendar year
  • Canadian digital services revenue was greater than CAN$10 million in the calendar year

This applies for any one of the 2022, 2023 or 2024 calendar years.

For full details: Registering for a digital services tax account

Program accounts with other federal government departments

Import and export (RM)

As of October 21, 2024, you need to register with the Canada Border Service Agency (CBSA) if you are a:

  • Importer
  • Exporter
  • Customs broker acting on behalf of a client

For details: Information on import or exporting.
